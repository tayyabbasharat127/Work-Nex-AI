Real-World Example
Pakistan Stock Exchange (PSX)
Step-by-Step Integration
PSX currently manages ~300 employees across trading operations, compliance, IT, and administration. Here is exactly what happens from Day 0 to a fully running WorkNex deployment.

📋 PSX Context

~300 employees · Departments: Trading Ops, Compliance, IT, HR, Finance, Admin · 3 shift patterns · Existing leave policy PDF (SECP-aligned) · Needs attendance linked to payroll · Board wants monthly workforce analytics in Power BI.

1

Day 0 — Organisation Registration (15 minutes)

PSX's IT head opens WorkNex, fills the registration form: org name, industry (Financial Services), country (Pakistan), and admin email. One POST to /billing/register atomically creates the PSX organisation, a 30-day free trial subscription, and the IT admin's ADMIN account — in a single database transaction.

✅ PSX org is live. IT admin receives email with licence key. No orphaned records possible. Trial starts immediately.
2

Day 0 — Configure Organisation Settings (10 minutes)

Admin logs in, opens Settings. Sets timezone to Asia/Karachi, working hours 09:00–17:00, late threshold 15 minutes, office GPS coordinates (I.I. Chundrigar Road, Karachi) and radius 200 metres, IP ranges for PSX's internal network, and enables leave automation.

✅ Attendance and leave rules are now PSX-specific — not generic defaults.
3

Day 1 — Department Setup (30 minutes)

Admin creates departments via the Users module: Trading Operations, Compliance, IT, HR, Finance, Administration. Each department is org-scoped and cannot be seen by any other tenant on the platform.

✅ Org chart exists in the system. Managers will later be scoped to their department.
4

Day 1 — Bulk Employee Onboarding

HR uploads employee data. For each employee the admin calls POST /auth/register (authenticated, ADMIN token) with role (EMPLOYEE or MANAGER), department, manager assignment, joining date, and employee ID. The system assigns the correct role with tenant isolation enforced server-side — no employee can self-assign ADMIN.

✅ 300 PSX employees have accounts. Each sees only their own org's data. MANAGER accounts for dept heads are created. Role escalation is blocked at the API layer.
5

Day 2 — Upload Leave Policy (20 minutes)

HR Manager goes to Policy Documents, uploads PSX's SECP-aligned leave policy PDF. The AI extracts rules: Annual Leave = 20 days, Sick = 10, Casual = 12, Maternity = 90, all with carry-forward rules. Confidence scores shown. HR approves the extracted rules in one click. From this moment, the leave automation engine uses PSX's exact policy — not generic defaults.

✅ Leave automation is now PSX-policy-compliant. No manual rule configuration needed. Future policy updates: just upload new PDF.
6

Week 1 — Employees Go Live

Each employee logs into WorkNex. From Day 1 of go-live they: check in/out via web (GPS-verified if outside office), view their attendance history, see their leave balances, ask the AI assistant "how many annual leaves do I have left?" and get a policy-grounded answer, apply for leave in 30 seconds, and receive notifications when their leave is decided.

✅ No training needed for employees. The AI assistant handles 80% of HR queries automatically — freeing PSX's 4-person HR team from repetitive questions.
7

Week 1 — Managers in Action

Department heads log in with MANAGER role. They see only their team's attendance and leave requests. When an employee applies for leave: the AI evaluates it against policy and the manager gets a recommendation ("APPROVE — within policy, no overlaps, 8 days balance remaining"). Manager approves or overrides with a note. Full audit trail recorded.

✅ Leave approval time drops from 3–5 days (email chain) to under 2 hours. Managers make better decisions with AI context.
8

Month 1 — Reports & Analytics for HR Director

HR Director opens the Analytics dashboard. Sees real-time: total headcount, attendance rate this month, late arrivals by department, pending leaves, leave by type breakdown. Generates a PDF-ready Attendance Report for payroll. Compliance team checks the audit log for any permission change or policy override.

✅ Month-end report that used to take 3 days in Excel is now live at all times — one click to generate, one click to export CSV.
9

Month 1 — Performance Visibility for MD/CEO

The ML model has been scoring employees silently since Week 1 — attendance rate, late count, leave patterns, working hours vs org average. The CEO logs into Power BI via WorkNex's embed token and sees a board-ready workforce health dashboard: top performers, at-risk employees by department, turnover prediction, department attendance benchmarks.

✅ The Board has objective, real-time workforce data for the first time. Performance conversations shift from "I feel Ahmed has been slacking" to "Ahmed's risk score is 74/100 — here's why."
10

Ongoing — Enable 2FA for Compliance

PSX's Compliance team (required by SECP for system access) enables TOTP 2FA on their accounts. They scan the QR code in any authenticator app. From that point, login requires both password and a 6-digit rotating code. No hardware tokens needed. Full audit log of every login.

✅ Compliance with SECP's cybersecurity guidance. No additional infrastructure. Built into WorkNex.
11

Month 3 — Upgrade from Trial to Paid Plan

Trial ends after 30 days. PSX's finance team logs into the Billing section, reviews the invoice, and upgrades to the Business plan (up to 500 employees, all modules). License key updated. Subscription is active. The entire data history — attendance, leave, performance — carries forward with zero disruption.

✅ No migration, no data loss. PSX is now a paying tenant on WorkNex AI SaaS.

🏗️ Architecture Principle: Tenant Isolation

Every database table has an organizationId column. Every API call through the backend middleware attaches the calling user's organizationId from their JWT. Queries are automatically scoped. It is architecturally impossible for Org A to read Org B's data — not just a UI restriction, enforced at the ORM layer.
